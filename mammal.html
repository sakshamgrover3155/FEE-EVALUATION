LION

The lion is a large, powerful carnivorous mammal belonging to the cat family, Felidae. Known as the "king of the jungle," lions are native to Africa and parts of Asia. They have short, tawny fur, with male lions distinguished by their majestic manes. Lions are social animals, living in groups called prides, which usually consist of females, cubs, and a few males. They primarily hunt large herbivores like zebras, buffalo, and antelope. Lions are known for their strength, speed, and roar.

ELEPHANT

Elephants are the largest land mammals on Earth, belonging to the family Elephantidae. They are known for their massive bodies, long trunks, large ears, and tusks made of ivory. Elephants use their trunks for various tasks like breathing, grasping objects, and drinking water. They are herbivores, feeding on grass, leaves, fruit. Elephants are highly social animals, living in groups called herds. Found in Africa and Asia, elephants play a key role in their ecosystems but are threatened by poaching and habitat destruction.

BEAR

Bears are large, powerful mammals belonging to the family Ursidae. They are found in North America, Europe, Asia, and parts of South America. Bears have thick fur, strong limbs, and sharp claws, which help them climb, dig, and hunt. Most species are omnivorous, eating a wide variety of foods such as plants, berries, fish, and small mammals. Some species, like the polar bear, are more carnivorous, while the panda feeds mostly on bamboo. Bears hibernate during winter and are known for their solitary nature.

TIGER

The tiger is the largest species of wild cat, known for its striking orange coat with black stripes. Tigers are solitary and territorial animals found mainly in Asia, from dense forests to grasslands and swamps. They are powerful predators that rely on stealth and strength to hunt prey such as deer, wild boar, and buffalo . Tigers are excellent swimmers and can cover large distances in search of food. Sadly, they are endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.

WOLF

The wolf is a large, carnivorous mammal that belongs to the dog family, Canidae. Known for their intelligence and pack behavior, wolves live and hunt in groups, often taking down prey like deer, elk, and smaller mammals. They have thick fur, which helps them survive in various habitats, from forests to tundras. Wolves communicate using vocalizations like howls, as well as body language and scent marking. Native to North America, Europe, and Asia, wolves are apex predators and play a crucial role in maintaining ecological balance.
